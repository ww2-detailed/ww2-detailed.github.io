World War 2: Detailed

Detailed timeline of World War 2

This is a detailed timeline of major events that happened during World War 2.

1939

September 1: The Second Sino-Japanese war in East Asia rages on.

September 1: Shortly before 6.00 am CET, the Wehrmacht, together with Slovak units, cross into Poland, beginning the invasion of Poland.

September 2: Britain and France issues an ultimatum on Germany, demanding that Germany backs down.

September 3: Following refusal, Britain and France declare war on Germany, marking the official start of World War 2.

September 17: The Soviet Union invades eastern Poland, in accordance with the Molotov-Ribbentrop Pact.

September 27: Warsaw, the capital of Poland, surrenders to German forces.

October 6: The last Polish resistance in the invasion of Poland ends with the surrender of the Modlin Fortress.

November 30: The Soviet Union invades Finland, starting the Winter War.

December 14: The League of Nations condemns the Soviet invasion of Finland, framing the USSR as the aggressor and thus expelling them from the League.

1940

January 7: Britain begins food rationing.

January 10: A German plane carrying plans for the planned invasion of France crashes in Belgium. This is known as the Mechelen Incident.

February 5: Britain and France cuts off the iron trade route between Norway and Germany, angering Germany.

April 9: The Wehrmacht lands in Oslo, starting the Norwegian campaign.

May 10: Wehrmacht troops cross into neutral Benelux, bypassing the Maginot line. Churchill is appointed Prime Minister of the United Kingdom upon the resignation of Chamberlein.

May 25: Allied troops are encircled in Dunkirk.

May 26: The Dunkirk evacuation begins, with Allied troops being evacuated across the English Channel. This is codenamed Operation Dynamo.

June 10: Italy declares war on France and Britain. Norway surrenders after 2 months of combat.

June 14: Paris is captured by the Wehrmacht.

June 17: The French government flees Paris and relocates to Vichy.

June 21: Soviet coups in the Baltic states take place; this would pave the way for the later annexation of the Baltic states by the USSR.

June 22: France signs the armistice of Compi√®gne, ending hostilities between the new Vichy government and Germany.

June 26: The Soviet Union demands Bessarabia and Northern Bukovina from Romania.

June 28: The Soviets occupy Bessarabia and Northern Bukovina, annexing them in the process.

July 10: The Battle of Britain begins as the German Luftwaffe starts bombing British cities.

August 17: Hitler declares a naval blockade of the British isles.

August 30: Second Vienna Award: Northern Transylvania is awarded to Hungary by Germany and Italy.

September 6: King Carol abdicates the Romanian throne in favor of his son Michael; Ion Antonescu consolidates power.

September 9: General Mario Berti orders the invasion of Egypt from Cyrenaica.

September 27: The Tripartite Pact is signed by Germany, Italy, and Japan. An informal name, 'Axis', emerges.

October 28: Italy declares war on Greece and invades them through Northern Epirus. Hitler is angered by this.
